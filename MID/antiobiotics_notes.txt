protein synthesis inhibitors:
	- 30S (AT):
		- aminoglycosides:
			- gentamicin, tobramycin, amikacin, streptomycin
			- prevent initiation of translation
			- cidal
			- resistance: enzymatic modification (plasmid or transposon)
			- forms: PE
			- uptake: water soluble
			- toxicity: nephro, ototo
			- metabolized: excreted to urine
			- uses:
				- actual: severe
				- GP: synergy with some severe (staph, enterococcus)
				- GN: severe, aerobic (enterobacter, pseudomonas)
				- yersinia pestis
		

		- tetracyclines (very broad spectrum):
			- doxycycline, tetracycline, etc
			- blocks tRNA binding, translation
			- static
			- resistance: uptake + e pumps (plasmid)
			- forms: PO, IV exist
			- uptake: lipophilic, high CNS penetration
			- toxicity: GI (common), photosensitivity, liver, teeth staining, allergic,  
			- metabolized: feces (doxy), liver (mino)
			- uses:
				- actual: skin + soft tissue
				- GP: staph (+ MRSA), strep, entero
				- GN: e. coli, and some (enterobacteria, haemophilus, neisseria, vibrio)
				- spirochetes, rickettsiae, chlamydia, mycoplasma
				- upgrades:
					- tigecycline (IV) + eravacycline: VRE + carbapenem R enterobacteria
						- black box warning


	- 50s (macro, lincos, oxa):
		- macrolides (broad spectrum):
			- azithromycin, clarithromycin, erythromicin
			- block peptide translocation
			- static
			- resistance: uptake, e-pumps, mutant rRNA (erm gene)
			- forms: PO, PE
			- uptake: good lungs, no CNS
			- toxicity: GI, long QT, P450 inhibitor
			- metabolized: bile excretion
			- uses:
				- actual: community pneumonia, pertussis, otitis, GN + CT
				- GP: strep+, some staph
				- GN: b. pertussis, n. campylobacter, legionella, haemophilus
				- other: mycoplasma, ureaplasma, chlamydia
				- upgrades:
					- azi/clarithro:
						- expanded GN: salmonella, shigella, campylobacter, pylori


		- lincosamides:
			- clindamycin
			- block translation midway, close to macros
			- static
			- resistance: inducible
			- forms: PO, PE
			- toxicity: diarrhea
			- uses:
				- actual: TSST, anaerobic when penicillin allergy or resistance
				- GP: aerobic (+ MRSA) and anaerobes
				- GN: none


		- oxazolidinones:
			- linezolid
			- stops initiation
			- static
			- resistance: mutation rRNA
			- forms: PO, PE
			- uptake: excellent
			- toxicity:
				- neuropathy (+ optic)
				- thrombocytopenia + myelosuppression
				- lactic acidos (rare)
				- serotonin syndrome
			- metabolized: liver -> urine
			- uses:
				- actual: drug resistance GPs
				- GP: MRSA + VRE 
				- GN: no
				- other:
		

glycopeptides:
	- vancomycin
	- blocks d-ala d-ala
	- static
	- resistance: d-ala d-lac
	- forms: IV, PO (for c. diff, not absorbed)
	- uptake: CNS
	- toxicity: red man (histamine)
	- metabolized: kidney
	- uses:
		- actual: severe broad spectrum GPs + resistance
		- GP: MRSA, R s.pneumo, enterococci, clostridium, listeria, bacillus
		- GN: no
		- other:


miscellaneous:
	- flouroquinolones (broad spectrum)
		- ciprofloxacin, levofloxacin, moxifloxacin, delafloxacin
		- dna gyrase inhibitor
		- cidal
		- resistance: mutation, efflux, chromosomal
		- forms: PO, IV
		- uptake: excellent, CSF + bone
		- toxicity: long QT, joints, GI (nausea +), neuro, vascular, allergic, photosensitivity
		- metabolized: renal
		- uses:
			- actual: complicated UTIs, STIs, pencillin allergies, PO invasive
			- GP: strep, MSSA
			- gn: aerobic (enterobacteriaceae, neisseria, campylobacter, pseudomonas)
			- other: legionella, chlamydia, mycoplasma, anthrax

	- metronidazole (broad anaerobe):
		- diffuse -> reduced -> radicals -> damage DNA
		- cidal
		- resistance: pores
		- forms: PO, IV, topical
		- uptake: abscesses, CNS
		- toxicity: disulfram, metal taste, P450
		- metabolized:
		- uses:
			- actual:
			- GP: clostridia
			- GN: bacteroides, helicobacter
			- other:
		
	- trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole (broad spectrum)
		- blocks folate metabolism
		- cidal
		- resistance: uptake, affinity (plasmid)
		- forms: PO, IV
		- uptake: excellent
		- toxicity: allergic, GI (nausea +), stevens johnson syndrome
		- metabolized: liver -> urine
		- uses:
			- actual:
			- GP: strep, staph (+ MRSA), listeria
			- GN: enterobacteria, neisseria, haemophilus
			- other: chlamydia
		
	- daptomycin (resistant GPs)
		- punches hole in membrane w/ liphilic tail
		- cidal
		- resistance: altered phospholipids
		- forms: IV
		- uptake: not lungs
		- toxicity: myopathy (monitor CPK)
		- metabolized: kidneys
		- uses:
			- actual:
			- GP: VRE, MRSA, pencillin-resistant pneumo
			- GN: no
			- other:
		
	- polymixin (resistant GNs)
		- detergent
		- static
		- resistance: LPS modification
		- forms: PO, IV
		- uptake:
		- toxicity: nephro, neuro
		- metabolized:
		- uses:
			- actual:
			- GP: no
			- GN: enterobacteria, pseudomonas
			- other:


beta-lactams:
	- inhibit cell wall synthesis
	- cidal
	- penicillins:
		- resistance: pores
		- forms: PO, IV
		- uptake:
		- toxicity: seizures, diarrhea, c. diff, biliary sludge, nephro (I nephritis), cyopenias, allergies

	cephalosporins:
		- 1st:
			- cephalexin (PO), cefazolin (IV)
			- GP: strep, staph
			- GN: e. coli, klebsiella, proteus
			- use: skin + soft tissue, perioperative prophylaxis
		- 2nd:
			- cefuroxime
				- haemophilus + respiratory tract infections
			- cefoxitin, cefotetan
				- bacteroides, anaerobic
				- use: gut/pelvic surgery
		- 3rd:
			- cetriaxone, cefotaxime
				- CNS penetration
				- biliary + renal excretion -> sludge
			- ceftazidime: pseudomonas
		- 4th:
			- cefepime
			- CNS penetration
			- GP: strep, staph
			- GN: pseudomonas + (less ampC susceptible)
		- 5th:
			- ceftaroline
			- 3rd gen + MRSA + resistant pneumococci

		- uses:
