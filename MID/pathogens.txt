vector borne pathogens:
	- rickettsia rickettsii
		- rocky mountain spotted fever
		- vector
		- specifics:
			- small GN coccobacilli
			- obligate intracellular
			- weak LPS, mininal peptidoglycan
		- vector: ixodidae ticks, american dog ticks
		- reservoir: mammals
		- symptoms
			- 1 week incubation -> flu symptoms
			- 2-5 days later -> macular eruption on appendages, centripetal rash (+hands, +feet)
		- pathogenesis: lymphatics -> phagocytosed -> fission in cytosol -> damage -> vascular leakage
		- tx: doxycycline

	- rickettsialpox:
		- r. akari
		- vector: mites
		- reservoir: mice
		- symptoms: pox, lymphadenopathy
		- dx: nothing, if bad -> doxycycline

	- epidemic typhus:
		- r. prowazekii
		- vector: human body louse
		- reservoir: humans
		- centrifugal petechial rash (no face, palms, soles)
		- multisys organ failure (fatal 5-40%)

	- ehrlichioses:
		- flu like
		- dx: acute PCR, sensitive -> serologies +4x
		- tx: doxycycline
		- pathogenesis:
			- obligate intracellular GN coccoid
			- morulae (spheres in phagocytome)
		- anaplasma phagocytophilum:
			- north, granulocytes (neutrophils)
			- vector: ixodes, scapularis + pacificus
			- reservoir: white-footed mice
		- ehrlichia chaffeensis:
			- shallow south monocytic
			- vector: lone star tick (ixodes)
			- reservoir: white-tailed deer
	- lyme:
		- borrelia burgdorferi, spirochete
		- vector: I. scapularis nymphs
		- reservoir: white footed mice, small mammals
		- science:
			- OspC variant -> increased human transmission
			- OspA -> hematogenous spread (binds plasminogen)
			- DbpA + DbpB -> bind collagen -> ECM of joints, heart, C/PNS
		- dx: EIA or IFA -> western blot
		- tx: doxycycline, cardiac/C/PNS -> ceftriaxone

streptococcus:
	- GP catalase negative
	- pyogenes:
		- β hemolytic, bacitracin S
		- pathogenesis:
			- M protein (inhibits complement, causes virulence) -> rheumatic fever
			- capsule: inflammation
			- adhesins (protein F1 and lipoteichoic acid) -> adhere to epithelia
			- virulence: M proteins, hyaluronate capsule, exotoxins
		- syndromes:
			- asymptomatic: common
			- pharyngitis: no cough, coryza, conjuctivitis, cough, diarrhea
			- poststrep glomerulonephritis: anti-streptolysin O
			- toxic shock like: exotoxins A-C
				- necrotizing fasciitis
			- rheumatic fever:
				- jones criteria: 2 major or 1 major + 2 minor
		- dx: culture best, rapid strep tests
		- tx: penicillin VK + clindamycin (or other protein synthesis inhibtiors)

		- α hemolytic, optochin S

			- phosphorylcholine C - polysaccharide

			- adhesins: choline binding proteins, A -> adhere to type II cells
			- IgA protease
			- capsule -> anti-phagocytic
		- tx: often pencillin resistance
			- PCV 13 vaccine (protein conjugate)

	- clostridium:
		- GP rods
		- colonize: GI everywhere
		- pathogenesis:
			- spore formation (hearty)

		- difficile:
			- pathogenesis:
				- enterotoxin A: chemotaxis, fluid, + hemorrhagic necrosis
				- cytotoxin B: polymerization of actin -> cytoskeleton loss
			- syndromes:
				- asymptomatic
				- antibiotic assoc. diarrhea
				- pseudomembranous colitis
			- dx:
				- ELISA for toxin A/B, PCR for toxin B
				- culture
			- tx:
				- fidaxomicin (alt. vancomycin PO or metronidazole PO/IV)
				- IV Ig
				- fecal transplant
				- bezlotozumab: antibody for toxin B

		- perfringens:
			- colonize: GI humans + animals
				- type A in soil, B-E in GI
			- pathogenesis:
				- α-toxin: lyse endothelial + RBCs -> vascular permeability
				- β-toxin: necrotizing activity
				- enterotoxin: increased intestinal membrane permeability
			- syndromes:
				- gastroenteritis
				- cellulitis, fascitis, myonecrosis (gas gangrene)

		- tetanus:
			- colonize: soil + animal GI
			- pathogenesis:
				- spore in wound
				- tetanospasmin: heat-labile neurotoxin -> blocks GABA
			- syndromes:
				- tetanus:
					- generalized:
						- bulbar + paraspinal muscles
						- autonomic symptoms
					- cephalic: only CNs
					- localized: only local
					- neonatal: infected umbilical stump -> general
			- tx: vaccine + metronidazole

		- botulinum:
			- colonize: soil + water
			- pathogenesis:
				- toxins A + B (cans), E (fish), F
				- blocks acetylcholine release
			- syndromes:
				- botulism:
					- blurred vision, dilatred pupils, dry mouth, constipation
					- bilateral peripheral muscle weakness
					- types:
						- foodborne: onset 1-2 days
						- infant: honey + syrups, onset: 3-10 days
				- wound botulism
				- asymptomatic
			- dx:
				- bilateral CN palsies: diplopia, dysphonia, dysarthria, dysphagia
				- intact mentation
				- bilateral flaccid paralysis
				- bioassay for toxin
				- EM nerve tests
			- tx:
				- supportive
				- metronidazole or penicillin
				- botulinum Ig (BIG)
				- botulism antitoxin heptavalent (HBAT) Ig

enteric bacteria:
	- cholera
		- facultative anaerobe GN bent rod w/ 1 flagellum
		- pathogenesis:
			- O1 and O139 cause disease
			- cholera toxin (CT): inhibits CFTR (not SGLT)
			- toxin-coregulated pilus: colonizing factor
			- ToxR: virulence regulator
			- inoculum: 10^5-10^8
			- protective against: stomach acid, LPS targetted IgA/G, vaccine

		- onset: 1-2 days (2 hour - 5 days)
		- duration: 7-14 days
		- symptoms:
			- asymptomatic (75%)
			- watery diarrhea (20%)
			- severe watery diarrhea, vomiting (5%)
		- tx:
			- IV / ORS
			- doxycycline / quinolone / macrolide


	- e. coli
		- facultative anaerobe GN rod +pili, +flagella
		- lactose fermenting
		- types:
			- enterotoxigenic (ETEC):
				- onset: 1-2 days
				- duration: 3-5 days
				- pathogenesis:
					- heat-stable (STa): increase cGMP
					- heat labile (LT): same mech as CT
			- enterohemorrhagic (EHEC):
				- pathogenesis:
					- STX 1/2: bind 28S ribosome -> hemorrhagic colitis
					- systemic STX -> endothelial damage, prothrombotic, proinflammation -> HUS
					- HUS: hemolytic uremic syndrome
						- glomerular epithelial damage from STX (2)
						- fragged RBCs, CNS symptoms, thrombocytopenia, AKI, HTN
						- 15% children

	- shigella
		- GN rod, non-motile
		- onset: 12 hours
		- duration: 7 days
		- inoculum: <200
		- symptoms:
			- watery diarrhea
			- initial: cramping, abdominal pain, fever,
			- main: severe abdominal pain, tenesmus, dysentery
		- types: many, dysenteriae most virulent (A1)
		- pathogenesis:
			- move along GI -> destruction, abscesses, ulcers
			- stays within mucosa
			- invades through macrophages -> evades phagosome
			- STX: blocks 60s ribosome
			- possible HUS

	- salmonella
		- GN rod
		- pathogenesis:
			- Sips/Ssps to induce phagocytosis by M cells
			- SPI 1/2 islands
			- replicates in phagosome
		- types:
			- typhoidal
				- s.typhi / paratyphi
				- human fecal-oral
				- onset: 5-21 days
				- duration: 4-8 weeks
				- pathogenesis: macrophage replication -> lymphatics -> spleen, liver, marrow
				- syndromes:
					- enteric fever:
						- rose spots (30%), hepatosplenomegaly (50%)
						- intestianl perforation, abscesses, endocarditis
						- relapse 10%

			- non-typhoidal
				- chickens + all animals
				- onset: 6-48 hours
				- duration: 3-7 day
				- pathogenesis: kills macrophages, limited to epithelium
				- syndromes:
					- gastroenteritis: cramping, fever, bloody diarrhea
					- bacteremia (quick onset)
					- tissue infection: arterial, cholystitis, osteomyelitis, arthritis
