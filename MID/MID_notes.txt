L17: sexually transmitted infections:
	main:
	- STI general symptoms:
		- discharge: GC, CT, mycoplasma
		- ulceration: HSV, syphilis
		- suprapubic/testicular/perineal discomfort, join pain: all
		- urethritis:
			- dysuria
			- urethral pruritis
			- discharge
			- dx:
				- gram stain > 2 WBCs/hpf
				- positive leukocyte esterase or > 10 WBC/hpf on first urine void

	- gonnoccocal infections:
		- 600k in US
		- signs:
			- 2-8 days incubation
			- abrut onset
			- severe dysuria
			- more purulent discharge

		- neisseria gonorrhea (GC):
			- oxidase positive
			- gram negative
			- intracellular diplococci (kidney bean shape)
			- infects mucus secreting columnar/cuboidal epithelial cells

			- resevoir: asymptomatic people
			- virulence factors:
				- pili: attach/penetrate non-ciliated host cells
					- inhibit phagocytosis
				- Opa: bind CD66 on epithelium -> internalization
				- LOS: attach epithelium, release TNF-Î±
				- Ng-OMPA: adhesion + invasion
				- PorB: interferes with degranulation of PMNS
					- prevents phagolysosome
					- prevents complement killing
				- RMP: inhibit host antibodies

			- diagnosis:
				- gram stain 98% sensitivity
				- NAAT: preferred testing
				- culutre: for resistance
			- clinical symptoms:
				- w/ penis:
					- dysuria, purulent discharge
				- w/ cervix: discharge, dysuria, vaginal bleeding, abdominal pain

			- disseminated GC:
				- 1-3% patients
				- risks: female, menstruation, C5-C8 deficiency
				- symptoms:
					- fever, pustular skin lesions
					- migratory/asymmetric polyarthralgias
					- hepatitis/endocarditis, etc

			- treatment:
				- ceftriaxone 500mg IM x 1
					- double if > 150kg
					
					- gentamicin/azythromycin if allergic
					- cefixime 800 mg for PO
				- doxycycline 100 mg x 2 per day x 7 if chlamydia


	- non-gonnococcocal infections:
		- signs:
			- 7-14 days incubation
			- mild dysuria
			- less volume, mucoid discharge

	- testing:
		- HIV every 3-6 mos for MSM, at risk
		- pregnant women:
			- test for syphilis
			- HCV
