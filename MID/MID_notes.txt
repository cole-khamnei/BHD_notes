L8_sepsis:
	- sepsis:
		- sepsis: hypotx + infection
		- septic shock: sepsis + lactate > 2 +  hypotx w/ IV fluids
		- 1/3 rule: 1/3 bacteremic and 1/3 all cultures negative
		- pathogens:
			- GPs: staph aureus + strep pneumo
			- GNs: e. coli, klebsiella, pseudomonas
		- pathogenesis:
			- upregulation inflammasome
			- increased coagulation + decreased anticoag -> DIC
			- hypotx + vasodilation + RBC deformity -> hypoperfusion -> hypoxia -> capillary leak
			- high lactate: caused by hypoperfusion + endogenous epinephrine
		- risks: chronic, bimodal age
		- labs:
			- increased WBCs, procalcitonin, lactate, ALT/LST
			- hypo/hyper glycemia
			- ileus
		- tx:
			- IV antibiotics
			- vasopressors: nor epi then vasopressin, dopamine
			- IV crystalloid
			- steroids?

L10_UTIs:
	- upper UT: pyelonephritis -> perinephric abscess (late)
	- lower UT: cystitis, urethritis, prostatitis
	- uncomplicated UTI:
		- most common
		- types:
			- simple cystitis
			- pyelonephritis w/o stones/pregnancy/obstruction/diabetes
		- pathogens:
			- e. coli (80%)
			- staph saprophyticus (11%)
			- klebsiella (3%)

	- complicated UTIs:
		- increased risk / susceptibility
		- pathogens:
			- e. coli (30%)
			- enterococcus (22%)
			- pseudomonas (20%)
			- staph epidermis (15%)
	- recurrent UTIs:
		- 2+ in 6 mo or 3+ in 12 mo
		- give prophylaxis: 2+ UTIs within 6 mo and no successful antibiotic measures
			- also can infrequently do antiseptic methenamine

	- cystitis:
		- dysuria, urinary urgency/freq, hematuria
		-tx:
			- TMP-SMX: if not resistant
			- nitrofurantoin
			- fosfomycin (single dose)

	- pyelonephritis:
		- fever, sweating, nausea/vomiting, flank pain
		- dysuria, urinary urgency/freq
		- collect urine culture
		- tx:
			- TMP/SMX, something specific

	- children:
		- < 2 yo: enuresis, fever, poor weight gain
		- > 3 yo: adult sx

	- dx (urine):
		- pyuria or RBCs
		- presence of bacteria (> 10^5 / mL)
			- asymptomatic: treat if pregnant or undergoing urological surgery
		- dipstick: nitrate to nitrite, leakocyte esterase test

	- risks (female): short urethra, diaphragm, spermicide

	- acute bacterial prostatitis:
		- sx:
			- fever, perineal + back pain, urinary retention
		- dx:
			- warm, swollen tender prostate
			- pyuria
			- urine culture
		- pathogens:
			- GN enterics + enterococci
			- nsaids + antibiotics

L13: respiratory infections:
	- community associated pneumonia:
		- pathogens:
			- bacteria (30-50%)
				- strep pneumonia
				- haemophilus influenza
				- s. aureus
				- s. pyogenes
				- moraxella catarrhalis
			- atypicals (>15%)

L17: sexually transmitted infections:
	main:
	- STI general symptoms:
		- discharge: GC, CT, mycoplasma
		- ulceration: HSV, syphilis
		- suprapubic/testicular/perineal discomfort, join pain: all
		- urethritis:
			- dysuria
			- urethral pruritis
			- discharge
			- dx:
				- gram stain > 2 WBCs/hpf
				- positive leukocyte esterase or > 10 WBC/hpf on first urine void

	- gonnoccocal infections:
		- 600k in US
		- signs:
			- 2-8 days incubation
			- abrut onset
			- severe dysuria
			- more purulent discharge

		- neisseria gonorrhea (GC):
			- GN oxidase positive
			- intracellular diplococci (kidney bean shape)
			- infects mucus secreting columnar/cuboidal epithelial cells
			- resevoir: asymptomatic people
			- virulence factors:
				- pili: attach/penetrate non-ciliated host cells
					- inhibit phagocytosis
				- Opa: bind CD66 on epithelium -> internalization
				- LOS: attach epithelium, release TNF-Î±
				- Ng-OMPA: adhesion + invasion
				- PorB: interferes with degranulation of PMNS
					- prevents phagolysosome
					- prevents complement killing
				- RMP: inhibit host antibodies

			- diagnosis:
				- gram stain 98% sensitivity
				- NAAT: preferred testing
				- culutre: for resistance
			- clinical symptoms:
				- w/ penis: dysuria, purulent discharge
				- w/ cervix: discharge, dysuria, vaginal bleeding, abdominal pain

			- disseminated GC:
				- 1-3% patients
				- risks: female, menstruation, C5-C8 deficiency
				- symptoms:
					- fever, pustular skin lesions
					- migratory/asymmetric polyarthralgias
					- hepatitis/endocarditis, etc

			- treatment:
				- ceftriaxone 500mg IM x 1
					- gentamicin/azythromycin if allergic
					- cefixime 800 mg for PO
				- doxycycline 100 mg x 2 per day x 7 if chlamydia

	- non-gonnococcocal infections:
		- signs:
			- 7-14 days incubation
			- mild dysuria
			- less volume, mucoid discharge

		- chlamydia trachomatis:
			- obligate intracellular parasite
			- similar to GN, but no cell wall
			- most frequent STI
			- dx: NAAT, serology for LGV
			- tx: doxycycline or azithromycin
			- pathogenesis:
				- elementary body (spreader)
				- reticulate body (breeder)
			- types:
				- A-C: endemic
				- D-K: GU infections
				- L1-3: lymphogranuloma venereum
			- syndromes:
				- asymptomatic (most)
				- urethritis, prostatisis, proctitis
				- PID
				- reactive arthritis: reiter's syndrome:
					- conjuctivitis, urethritis, skin lesions
				- lymphogranuloma venereum:
					- early: painless ulcer 3-30 days
					- middle: tender inguinal lymphadenopathy (constitutional Sx)
					- late (mo - yrs): hard masses, urethral/rectal strictures

	- PID:
		- fitz-hugh-curtis: perihepatitis
		- risks: multiple partners, < 20 yo
		- signs: fever, discharge, CRP or ESR, cervical motion tenderness
		- tx: metronidazole + ceftriaxone + doxycycline

	- herpes:
		- 1-4 people has HSV-2 antibodies
		- primary stage 21 days
		- dx: PCR, serologies
		- tx: acycolvir, famciclovir, valacyclovir (decreases severity)

	- haemophilus decreyi:
		- GN coccobacilli
		- causes HIV risk
		- sx: *painful* ulcer, ragged edges, yellow exudate
		- tx: azythromycin, ceftriaxone, ciprofloxacin, erythromycin

	- granuloma inguinale (donovanosis):
		- klebsiella granulomatosis
		- GN bacillus
		- locations: equatorial line, southern Africa, south america
		- sx: painless subcutaneous nodules
		- dx: dark donovan bodies on smear of lesion
		- tx: 3 weeks - azythromycin, doxycycline, erythromycin, bactrim

	- testing:
		- HIV every 3-6 mos for MSM, at risk
		- pregnant women:
			- test for syphilis
			- HCV
